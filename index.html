Damsel 2024

"Damsel" is a 2024 fantasy film starring Millie Bobby Brown as Elodie, a noblewoman trapped in a marriage with Prince Henry. On her wedding day, Elodie is dragged into a cave as a sacrifice to pay off the royal family's debt. Trapped with a fire-breathing dragon, Elodie must rely on her intelligence and determination to survive. This film is a compelling tale of courage and survival.

Lift 2024

Cyrus is a renowned international thief who leads a heist crew consisting of Denton, pilot Camila, hacker Mi-Sun, safecracker Magnus, and engineer Luke. They pull off two simultaneous thefts: stealing a Van Gogh painting in London and staging the fake kidnapping of famous NFT artist N8 in Venice. The stolen painting is sold on the black market for $20 million to purchase N8’s NFTs. After the “kidnapping,” N8’s NFT value skyrockets to $89 million. Interpol agent Abby Gladwell, Cyrus’s former romantic interest, gathers evidence implicating Denton in the theft and arrests him. However, instead of prosecuting the crew, Abby’s superior, Commander Huxley, coerces them into helping capture billionaire Lars Jorgenson. Jorgenson plans to profit from a massive flooding in Europe orchestrated by the hacker group Leviathan. The crew devises a daring plan to steal $500 million in gold bullion from a passenger plane in mid-air to prevent Jorgenson’s scheme. This film combines elements of action, comedy, and crime, offering a thrilling ride with a touch of humor and romance.

Society of the Snow

On October 13, 1972, the plane carrying the Old Christians Club rugby team and their supporters to a game in Santiago, Chile, meets disaster when it crashes into a glacier. Of the 45 passengers, 29 initially survive the crash. However, they face injury, disease, and an avalanche in the weeks that follow, with more succumbing to the harsh conditions. In the face of extreme adversity, trapped in one of the planet’s most hostile environments, the survivors are compelled to make unthinkable choices, including resorting to cannibalism of the deceased, to stay alive.Despite the dire circumstances, the group’s unity and the cooperative spirit they developed through rugby, along with their spiritual faith, become their greatest assets. The film explores the depths of human resilience and the strength of the human spirit in times of crisis.The cast includes Enzo Vogrincic, Matías Recalt, and Agustín Pardella, among others, mostly newcomers from Uruguay and Argentina. “Society of the Snow” was released on Netflix on January 4, 2024, after closing the 80th Venice International Film Festival. It received positive reviews and won several awards, including Best Picture and Best Director at the 38th Goya Awards. It was also nominated for Best International Feature Film and Best Makeup and Hairstyling at the 96th Academy Awards.

Rebel Moon

The film opens with the arrival of Atticus Noble, the sadistic admiral of the Imperium, on the moon of Veldt, which is under the dominion of the Motherworld. Noble demands the village’s surplus grain, but when the village leader Sindri refuses, Noble kills him. Noble then gives the villagers ten weeks to prepare the grain, threatening their survival. In response, Kora, a villager and former soldier, along with a defected “Jimmy” robot, kills the Imperium soldiers left behind to oversee the harvest. She warns the villagers of the impending massacre upon Noble’s return and begins to assemble a defense. The film follows Kora’s efforts to unite a band of warriors from different worlds, each seeking redemption and revenge, to make a stand against the tyrannical ruling force. “Rebel Moon” features an ensemble cast including Sofia Boutella, Djimon Hounsou, Ed Skrein, Michiel Huisman, Doona Bae, Ray Fisher, Charlie Hunnam, and Anthony Hopkins. It was released on Netflix on December 22, 2023, following a limited theatrical release on December 15, 2023. The film received generally negative reviews from critics and an R-rated extended cut is set for release in mid-2024. A sequel, “Rebel Moon – Part Two: The Scargiver,” was released on April 19, 2024.

Family Switch

In Los Angeles, the Walker family is struggling to stay connected. Jess and Bill, along with their three children, CC, Wyatt, and Miles, find themselves at odds due to their different outlooks on life. As Christmas approaches, the family visits the Griffith Observatory to witness a rare planetary alignment. Amidst the tension, Jess and CC, as well as Bill and Wyatt, wish to understand each other better by walking in each other’s shoes. Their wish comes true in an unexpected way when a blinding flash at the observatory causes them to swap bodies. Jess and CC, as well as Bill and Wyatt, wake up to find themselves in each other’s bodies, leading to a series of comedic and heartwarming misadventures as they attempt to navigate life from a new perspective. The family must work together to reverse the switch before the end of the planetary alignment, learning valuable lessons about empathy and family bonds along the way. The film received mixed reviews from critics and explores themes of family unity and understanding through a humorous body-swapping scenario.

El Conde

“El Conde” is a darkly comedic and satirical film that takes a unique approach to the historical figure of Augusto Pinochet, reimagining him as a centuries-old vampire. The film traces his journey from a French soldier to a feared dictator in Chile, all while maintaining his vampiric nature. As the story unfolds, we see Pinochet, now known as “The Count,” grappling with the loss of his will to live after 250 years. His family, eager for their inheritance, and his loyal butler, who is also a vampire, become central to the plot as they navigate the complexities of their existence. The film takes a turn when a nun named Carmen is hired to audit the family’s wealth but is revealed to have ulterior motives. Her interactions with Pinochet lead to unexpected consequences, further entangling the characters in a web of power, desire, and immortality. “El Conde” premiered at the Venice International Film Festival, winning acclaim for its screenplay, and later received an Oscar nomination for its cinematography. Its release on Netflix coincided with the historical anniversary of Pinochet’s rise to power, adding a layer of significance to its reception.

Sex Education

Sex Education is a British teen sex comedy drama television series created by Laurie Nunn for Netflix. It follows the lives of teenagers and adults in the fictional town of Moordale as they contend with various personal dilemmas, often related to sexual intimacy. The ensemble cast includes characters like Otis Milburn, a student at Moordale Secondary School, who begins the series ambivalent about sex due to his single mother, Jean Milburn, being a sex therapist. Other key characters include Maeve Wiley, an intelligent and rebellious teen; Eric Effiong, Otis’s best friend and the gay son of Ghanaian-Nigerian immigrants; and Adam Groff, the headmaster’s son who struggles with self-loathing. The show explores themes of relationships, sexuality, and personal growth, all set against the backdrop of a school sex therapy clinic that Otis and Maeve set up. In the third season, the students return to school after summer break, and the new headteacher, Ms. Hope Haddon, introduces strict rules and regulations. The tension between Otis and Maeve continues, and the students witness public humiliation, leading to Hope’s resignation. However, their protest has unintended consequences, jeopardizing the school’s future. Meanwhile, Otis and Maeve finally come together, but Maeve’s acceptance into a program in the United States becomes a hurdle.
